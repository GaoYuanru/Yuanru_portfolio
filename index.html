About Me


My name is Yuanru Gao (高愿濡) and I currently live in London, UK. I graduated from the Bartlett Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis, University College London, with a Master's degree in Urban Spatial Science. I also have a Bachelor of Science degree in Geographic Information Science from Shandong Jianzhu University. I specialise in geospatial analysis, data visualisation, and web design, and have strong programming experience (Python, R, Java). During my undergraduate studies, I published a high-quality Chinese paper and obtained a software patent. During my master's degree, I completed an urban regeneration visualisation project and a remote sensing study diary project.

Besides, I am also a dancer, specialising in Chinese traditional dance, with more than 10 years of dancing experience. As an art talent, I have participated in many national competitions, and I have also filmed two dance-related microfilms Maps and Brave.

My interests are in spatial interaction and analysis as well as artistic expression. At this stage, I am committed to combining my academic knowledge with my interests to turn them into products or technologies that can help people have a better experience.

My Work - Academics

Visualisation of Urban Regeneration in
Great Manchester (Webpage Link)

Investigated Manchester's history and analyzed house price data over time. Used Mapbox, jQuery, JavaScript, Echarts, Google Map API and CesiumJS 3D to visualize the data, highlighting Manchester's urban regeneration and revitalization efforts.

3D GIS services based on
Android mobile device (Paper Link)

A 3D GIS service publishing method based on Android mobile is proposed, and several new service architectures are designed to implement the 3D tile data standard in 3D model service. Through experimental comparisons, this Android-based method shows superior performance and higher publishing efficiency compared to the Nginx-based static service middleware, which can better meet the current needs of complex 3D application environments. In this project, I was mainly responsible for data collection and cleaning, documentation and format calibration.

Remotely Sensing Learning Diary
(Webpage Link)

An introductory learning diary on remote sensing, created using GitHub Pages, Quarto and RS tools like Google Earth Engine, SNAP and ENVI.

Network path-based indoor location sensing and spatial relationship analysis
(Vedio Display)

The project mainly used GIS technology, GIS software SuperMap, Dijkstra's algorithm and genetic algorithm to create indoor maps and path analyses for the Beidong building of Shandong Jianzhu University, in order to study and demonstrate the best indoor navigation paths.

Internet + GIS research on Jinan Smart Spring System for Tourism
(Software Patent Certificate)

The project adopts technologies such as Python, JavaScript and Echarts to realise a comprehensive real-time information integration system for scenery areas. The system can collect, process and display all kinds of data about the scenery in real-time, including the water level of the spring, air temperature in the garden, number of tourists, and so on, to help tourists intelligently obtain real-time status and data changes of the scenery, and at the same time, it is also convenient for managers to carry out the overall coordination and management. In addition, the system also provides interactive discussion forums and ‘likes’ functions, so that tourists can share their experiences and provide feedback, which enhances the tourists' experience and the management efficiency of the scenic spot.

Spatial and temporal patterns of new urbanisation and coupled urban-rural integration and coordinated development in Shandong Province (Bachelor's Dissertation)

The development dynamics of new urbanisation and urban-rural integration in Shandong Province are explored, highlighting changes and patterns in different temporal and spatial dimensions. Through the expert scoring method and principal component analysis, the study depicts the development and evolution of these two dimensions in the cities of Shandong Province between 2000 and 2020. It was found that cities in Shandong Province showed significant spatial and temporal variability in the development levels of new urbanisation and urban-rural integration, with a spatial pattern of "high in the north-eastern Jiaodong and the central part of the province, and low in the south-eastern and western parts of the province".
